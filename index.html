2 The Timeline Fraud

When challenged in state court, the State claimed this information was disclosed in a 2006 report. A phantom report the prosecutor has yet produced to any court. But science proves that is a lie. The 2011 report comparison was based on reference profiles that were not even created until January 2008. You cannot disclose a comparison in 2006 for a profile that didn't exist for another two years. Most importantly, the comparison was not done until 2011; before that, any information consisted of worthless, unknown DNA profiles.

The Federal District Court found merit in this claim. Unlike the state court—which basically adopted the prosecution's lie—Judge McNulty did not fall for the false comparison between the 2006 and 2011 reports. Finding a "merit" in Elmo's claim, the Federal Court granted a stay to perfect the record, acknowledging the "stark conflict" in the State's timeline and the fact that the prosecutor never produced the phantom 2006 report in its opposition to deny the stay.
